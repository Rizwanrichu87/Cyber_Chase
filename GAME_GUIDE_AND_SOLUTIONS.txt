YOUR ROLE: Corporate Security Investigator

THE SITUATION:
On March 17, 2026 at 02:47 AM standard time, your company's main server was breached. The
attacker managed to:
- Extract the entire customer database
- Steal sensitive financial records  
- Install a permanent backdoor
- Partially cover their tracks

THE INVESTIGATION:
You have 5 suspects. All employees. All have different reasons they couldn't
have done it... or do they?

LEVEL 1: Access logs reveal suspicious IP activity. Marcus Chen had logged in
from outside the office. But his gym's security cameras show he was there.

Visa stamps and airline records confirm. Paula is cleared.

LEVEL 3: System logs show commands from Devon Patel's workstation. But airport
records show they were boarding a flight when the breach occurred.
Devon is cleared.

LEVEL 4: Firewall records show the breach originated from inside the network.
Iris Wagner was at work the whole time, witnessed by 8 colleagues.
Iris is cleared.

LEVEL 5: THE TWIST.
All suspects have alibis. All seem innocent. But one person remains:
LYRA VANCE. The quiet junior developer nobody paid attention to.

Server logs reveal they were logged in TWICE at the same time—impossible unless
they used advanced session hijacking techniques. Further investigation reveals:
- Cryptocurrency wallet with $500K transfer (March 12th)
- Burner phone traced to their location
- Motive: Passed over for promotion twice, negative performance review
- Method: Remote session hijacking while physically elsewhere

CASE CLOSED. But the real twist? You never suspected them because they were
so easy to overlook.
